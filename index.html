I was born on February 28th, 2002 in Hayward. I was mostly raised in Pleasanton, California, which is in the Bay Area. And thanks to my early travels around the world with my parents, much of my childhood was filled with adventure and exploration. By the age of fifteen, I had visited China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Tahiti, Taiwan, Thailand, and the United Kingdom. During my travels, I had the opportunity to watch multiple international films, where I learned what international unity means. As I entered high school, I started to develop my own views about film and entertainment as a whole.

People try to escape the hardships of the world by watching films. Someone has fun watching a movie and then moves on with their day, dealing with reality once again. Despite my old views, as I grew older and watched more movies and shows, I began to realize the true purpose of motion pictures, which started changing the way I saw film and the world. I find that the definition of entertainment has changed. To entertain also means to possibly allow yourself or someone to think about an idea, a hope, a feeling, and so on. Films are not just made to give their audience joy for twenty-five minutes to two hours but also to have them think about the realities of the movies‚Äô themes and messages. People watch to escape the world but also to be closer to themselves as people and reality. I watched more movies after every family trip. I wanted to see and always learned something from them in the end. Later on, I decided to expand my creativity and understanding of film by watching Japanese, Chinese, and Korean animations. After exploring many aspects of entertainment, my interest in filmmaking grew. This is when I reached the conclusion that I wanted to study motion pictures in college.

In hopes to impact audiences with personal and world messages, I pursued first-hand opportunities where I can learn more about filmmaking.
